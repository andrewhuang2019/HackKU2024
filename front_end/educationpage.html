Education


Practice Proper Hand Hygiene:

Wash your hands thoroughly with soap and water before preparing food, after handling raw meat, poultry, or seafood, after using the bathroom, and after touching pets.


Separate Raw Foods from Ready-to-Eat Foods:

Use separate cutting boards and utensils for raw meats, poultry, and seafood to avoid cross-contamination with ready-to-eat foods like fruits and vegetables.


Cook Foods to Safe Temperatures:

Use a food thermometer to ensure that foods are cooked to a safe internal temperature to kill harmful bacteria. Refer to safe cooking temperature guidelines for different types of foods.


Wash Fruits and Vegetables:

Rinse fresh fruits and vegetables under running water before eating, cutting, or cooking to remove dirt, bacteria, and pesticides.


Be Cautious with Leftovers:

Refrigerate or freeze leftover foods promptly. Reheat leftovers thoroughly to an internal temperature of 165°F (74°C) before consuming.
